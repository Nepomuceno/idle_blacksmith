Privacy Policy for Idle Blacksmith

Last updated: January 2026

Introduction

Idle Blacksmith ("we", "our", or "the game") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our mobile game.

Information We Collect

Idle Blacksmith does not collect any personal information.

The game stores your progress locally on your device. This data never leaves your device and is not transmitted to any servers.

Local Data Storage

The following data is stored locally on your device:

  • Game progress (gold, items forged, upgrades purchased)
  • Achievements unlocked
  • Settings preferences

This data is stored in your device's app data folder and can be deleted by uninstalling the game.

Third-Party Services

Advertising (Currently Disabled): The game may include optional advertising in future updates. If enabled, ads will be served through Google AdMob, which has its own privacy policy. You will be notified if advertising is enabled through an app update.

Children's Privacy

Idle Blacksmith is suitable for all ages. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. Since we don't collect any personal information from anyone, this applies equally to users of all ages.

Data Security

Since all game data is stored locally on your device, data security depends on your device's security settings. We recommend using device-level security features like passcodes or biometric authentication.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected in the "Last updated" date at the top of this policy. Continued use of the game after any changes constitutes acceptance of the new policy.

Platform-Specific Information

iOS (App Store)

On iOS devices, game data is stored in the app's sandboxed container and may be included in iCloud backups if you have iCloud backup enabled for this app.

Android (Google Play)

On Android devices, game data is stored in the app's private data directory.

Desktop (Steam)

On desktop platforms, game data is stored in your user data directory. Steam Cloud sync is not currently supported but may be added in future updates.
